# Agentic AI Foundation Case Study Guidelines

## **Overview**

The Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF) publishes case studies to show how member organizations are adopting open agentic AI standards — like MCP, goose, and AGENTS.md — to solve real business problems. These stories help the broader community learn from practical implementations and help participating organizations showcase their leadership.

There are two ways to participate:

**Written Case Study** — Open to any AAIF member organization. AAIF members may also provide a case study centered on a customer use case, with details about how they supported the implementation. All case studies must demonstrate at least one tangible business impact.

**Webinar Case Study** — AAIF member organizations can submit a case study in webinar format (a 20–30 minute recorded presentation with Q\&A) for publication on AAIF channels.

## **Strategic Focus**

We prioritize stories that go beyond "what was built" to explain the value unlocked. The strongest case studies demonstrate one or more of the following:

* **Agentic maturity:** Moving from simple chatbots or copilots to autonomous, goal-oriented systems that take action.

* **Interoperability:** Seamless integration between diverse models, tools, and environments via the Model Context Protocol (MCP) or AGENTS.md.

* **Measurable acceleration:** Quantitative proof of how agentic workflows reduced time-to-resolution, cut costs, or improved throughput.

### **Example Headlines That Work**

* "How \[Company\] Cut Customer Resolution Time by 60% with goose and MCP"

* "From Manual Triage to Autonomous Ops: \[Company\]'s Journey with AGENTS.md"

* "\[Company\] Reduced Deployment Errors by 45% Using MCP-Based Agent Orchestration"

## **What to Include**

*All case studies must demonstrate at least one tangible business impact of an AAIF project.*

### **Title & Summary**

* Use a clear, results-oriented headline (see examples above).

* Include a 2–3 sentence executive summary with key outcomes.

### **Company Overview**

* Briefly introduce the organization: size, industry, mission, and relevant technology strategy.

### **Challenge**

* Describe the specific technical or business problems the organization faced.

* Include measurable goals or pain points that motivated the initiative.

### **Solution**

* Detail the implementation with emphasis on why AAIF tools were chosen.

* Include architecture diagrams where applicable.

* Describe phases of adoption (e.g., pilot, scale, production).

### **Results**

* Quantifiable improvements: time saved, costs reduced, performance gains, error rates, etc.

* Developer productivity and team morale impacts.

* Before/after comparisons or metrics.

* Key lessons learned.

### **Future Plans**

* Upcoming initiatives or deeper integration with AAIF technologies.

* Potential for community contributions or open sourcing of tooling.

### **Quotes (Required)**

At least one quote must be included from someone who worked on or with the project. Engineers, SREs, product leads, or engineering managers help humanize the story and add credibility. Aim for 2–3 quotes distributed throughout the piece.

## **Writing & Format Guidelines**

**Tone:** Conversational and honest. Use the active voice. Avoid jargon-heavy corporate speak — if you wouldn't say it to a colleague over coffee, rephrase it.

**Length:** 750–1,000 words. Aim for the lower end; concise stories perform best.

**Section headers:** Use clear, results-oriented headers rather than generic labels like "Challenge" or "Solution." Good headers add interest, provide context, and improve discoverability.

**Visuals:** Include high-resolution architecture diagrams and/or high-quality headshots of featured contributors. Diagrams should be legible at web resolution.

**Call to action:** Every case study must end with a link to the relevant project's GitHub repository and a call to action to join the AAIF Discord community.

**Style notes:**

* Use "open source" (two words, no hyphen) and "agentic AI" as standard style.

* Break up long paragraphs with bullets to highlight key points, metrics, and successes.

* Tell the full journey — including challenges and roadblocks adds authenticity.

* Mention contributions, bug reports, or participation in AAIF working groups, events, and community meetups where relevant.

## **Eligibility & Neutrality**

**End user driven:** We prioritize stories told from the perspective of the end user — the organization implementing the technology — rather than the vendor.

**Vendor neutrality:** Case studies must focus on the use of AAIF projects (e.g., AGENTS.md, MCP, goose) rather than promoting a specific proprietary vendor's platform. Vendor products may be mentioned as part of the technical stack, but should not be the primary focus.

**Project scope:** Projects featured should be under the AAIF umbrella or demonstrate significant integration with AAIF standards.

## **Submission & Review Process**

The case study program follows a self-service partnership model. You bring the knowledge; we provide editorial support and guidance.

**Step 1 — Propose:** Submit your case study proposal [via the AAIF content intake form](https://form.asana.com/?k=Bq-lRvAXGzHQIXH_etPOxA&d=9283783873717) (select "Case Study" from the dropdown). Include the company name, AAIF project(s) used, and a 2–3 sentence summary of the story. We will let you know if your proposal is accepted. 

**Step 2 — Draft:** Write your case study following these guidelines. 

**Step 3 — Review:** The AAIF marketing team reviews for vendor neutrality, alignment with these guidelines, and grammatical quality. Highly technical pieces will require longer review time. If your submission doesn't meet the criteria, we'll provide specific feedback and you're welcome to resubmit.

**Step 4 — Approval:** The draft is shared with your communications, legal, and marketing teams as needed for sign-off.

**Step 5 — Publish & Promote:** Approved case studies are published on the AAIF website and may be amplified across AAIF channels, including the newsletter, social media, and Discord. We also optimize case studies for discoverability in AI-driven citation systems.

**Questions?** Contact the [case study/content team](mailto:asalkever@linuxfoundation.org) for guidance, feedback, or to discuss your proposal before submitting.
